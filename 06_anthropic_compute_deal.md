# SpaceX S-1: The Anthropic Compute Deal — Verified

**Source:** Space Exploration Technologies Corp. Form S-1, filed with the SEC on May 20, 2026 (preliminary prospectus, "SUBJECT TO COMPLETION"). Everything below is taken from the S-1 only — no outside sources. Each cited page was re-read from scratch to confirm the quote and the page before saving.

**Headline:** In May 2026 SpaceX entered into "Cloud Services Agreements" with **Anthropic PBC** to rent compute capacity across its **COLOSSUS** and **COLOSSUS II** data centers. The body of the prospectus states the price as **$1.25 billion per month through May 2029**; the financial-statement notes describe the identical agreement but **redact the rate**, saying only "a monthly fee." That disclosure gap is documented in Section 4.

---

## 1. The Deal — Exact Quote from the Business Summary (p. 14)

> "For example, in May 2026, we entered into Cloud Services Agreements with Anthropic PBC ("Anthropic"), an AI research and development public benefit corporation, with respect to access to compute capacity across COLOSSUS and COLOSSUS II. Pursuant to these agreements, the customer has agreed to pay us $1.25 billion per month through May 2029, with capacity ramping in May and June 2026 at a reduced fee. The agreements may be terminated by either party upon 90 days' notice. The customer will retain ownership and intellectual property rights in its content, AI models, and related data."

— S-1, business summary, **p. 14**.

The same paragraph appears verbatim (with "$1.25 billion per month through May 2029") in the MD&A on **p. 147** (the only textual difference is that p. 147 writes "Anthropic" without re-introducing the "(\"Anthropic\")" defined term, since it is already defined earlier).

## 3. COLOSSUS and COLOSSUS II — Glossary Definitions (p. v)

From the defined-terms glossary in the prospectus front matter, **p. v**:

> "'COLOSSUS' refers to our flagship data center, located on Paul R. Lowry Road in Memphis, Tennessee."

> "'COLOSSUS II' refers to our data centers in Memphis, Tennessee and in Southaven, Mississippi. These data centers are part of our coherent gigawatt-scale AI training cluster."

— S-1, defined-terms glossary, **p. v**.

These are the facilities whose compute capacity is rented to Anthropic under the agreements in Section 1. (Elsewhere the S-1 states COLOSSUS and COLOSSUS II "collectively provide approximately 1.0 gigawatt of compute," e.g., pp. 65 and 73, and that Grok 5 "is currently being trained at COLOSSUS II," p. 14.)

## 4. The Disclosure Gap — $1.25 Billion/Month Is in the Body, Redacted in the Notes

The same agreement is disclosed in two places with **different levels of specificity**. The **body** states the price; the **audited and interim financial-statement notes** do not.

### 4a. Body — states the rate ($1.25 billion per month)
- **p. 14** (business summary): "…the customer has agreed to pay us **$1.25 billion per month** through May 2029…"
- **p. 147** (MD&A): "…the customer has agreed to pay us **$1.25 billion per month** through May 2029…"

### 4b. Financial-statement notes — redact the rate ("a monthly fee")
Subsequent-events note titled "Cloud Services Agreement," which appears twice — once in the **annual** audited financial statements and once in the **interim** unaudited financial statements. Both read identically and **omit the dollar figure**:

> "On May 3, 2026, the Company entered into a cloud services agreement with Anthropic PBC, an AI research and development public benefit corporation, with respect to access to compute capacity. Pursuant to this agreement, the customer has agreed to pay **a monthly fee** through May 2029, with capacity ramping in May 2026 at a reduced fee. The agreement may be terminated by either party upon 90 days' notice. The customer will retain ownership and intellectual property rights in its content, AI models, and related data."

- **p. F-63** — subsequent-events note in the annual (audited) financial statements.
- **p. F-96** — subsequent-events note in the interim (unaudited, as of/for the three months ended March 31, 2026) financial statements.

### 4c. Side-by-side

| Field | Body (pp. 14, 147) | Notes (pp. F-63, F-96) |
|---|---|---|
| Price | **"$1.25 billion per month"** | **"a monthly fee"** (no figure) |
| Term | "through May 2029" | "through May 2029" |
| Signing date | "May 2026" | "On May 3, 2026" |
| Ramp | "May and June 2026 at a reduced fee" | "May 2026 at a reduced fee" |
| 90-day termination | yes | yes |
| Instrument (plural?) | "Agreements" (plural) | "agreement" (singular) |

**The gap, stated plainly:** an investor reading only the audited/interim financial statements (pp. F-63, F-96) would learn the agreement exists, its term (through May 2029), its signing date (May 3, 2026), and the 90-day termination right — but **not the $1.25 billion-per-month price**. The price is disclosed only in the narrative body of the prospectus (pp. 14 and 147). The notes also describe it in the **singular** ("a cloud services agreement"), whereas the body uses the **plural** ("Cloud Services Agreements").

## 5. Reconciliation / Precision Notes (from the re-read)

1. **$1.25 billion/month confirmed on both body pages.** Re-read p. 14 (footer "14") and p. 147 (footer "147") from scratch; the figure, the "through May 2029" term, the "May and June 2026" ramp, and the 90-day termination all appear word-for-word.
2. **Notes confirmed to omit the rate.** Re-read both subsequent-events notes; each says "a monthly fee" with no dollar amount, gives the May 3, 2026 signing date, the "through May 2029" term, and the 90-day termination.
3. **Page F-96 — footer caveat.** The F-63 note sits directly above the printed "F-63" page footer. The second note sits **just below** the printed "F-96" footer (the last F-page marker captured before the prospectus back cover) and is the final subsequent-events item in the interim financial statements; no further F-page footer appears before the back cover in the filing copy reviewed. Cited as **F-96** per the nearest printed page marker; the note is on or immediately adjacent to that page. The figure being redacted is identical to the F-63 note either way.
4. **Body uses "$1.25 billion per month"; no aggregate contract value is stated.** The S-1 does **not** disclose a single total contract value. A naive run-rate of $1.25B/month over the ~36 months from June 2026 through May 2029 would be ~$45 billion (**this is a derived back-of-envelope figure, NOT stated in the S-1**, and it ignores the reduced ramp-period fee in May/June 2026 and the 90-day cancellation right).
5. **Anthropic also appears as a competitor**, separately from this deal — e.g., listed among frontier-model competitors to Grok (p. 54 area and AI-competition risk factors). That is unrelated to the compute agreement documented here.

---

## 6. Bottom Line

SpaceX has agreed to sell Anthropic compute capacity on COLOSSUS and COLOSSUS II for **$1.25 billion per month through May 2029** (signed May 3, 2026; either party may terminate on 90 days' notice). The price is disclosed in the prospectus body (pp. 14, 147) but **redacted to "a monthly fee" in the financial-statement notes (pp. F-63, F-96)** — a real disclosure-specificity gap between the narrative and the audited/interim statements.
