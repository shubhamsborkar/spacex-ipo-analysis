# SpaceX S-1: Debt, Cash, and Lender Control — Verified

**Source:** Space Exploration Technologies Corp. Form S-1, audited consolidated financial statements (PCAOB-audited) for fiscal years ended December 31, 2025, 2024, 2023.

**Method:** Re-read the audited Balance Sheet (p. F-4), Note 10 (Debt, p. F-30 to F-34), Note 11 (Leases, p. F-35 to F-36), and the Statement of Shareholders' Equity (p. F-7) from scratch on the second pass.

**Headline:** SpaceX is **not under lender control as of Dec 31, 2025** — it is in compliance with all covenants, has more cash than total funded debt and finance leases (net cash of $(1.9)B), and shareholders' equity remains positive ($2.6B). However, there are two flags worth noting: (i) a 2026 technical default on the SpaceX Credit Facility when xAI was acquired, cured by a March 2, 2026 covenant waiver; and (ii) a 2029 maturity wall of $13.5B (61% of total principal), concentrated in the X B-1 and X B-3 term loans.

## 4. Covenants and Restrictive Provisions (Note 10)

**The S-1 does NOT disclose specific covenant ratios (e.g., debt/EBITDA, interest coverage, fixed-charge coverage).** It states only that the facilities contain "customary" covenants and confirms compliance. The following restrictions are disclosed:

### 4a. Compliance status (as of Dec 31, 2025)

- **SpaceX Credit Facility** (new $1.5B revolver, Feb 2025, matures Feb 7, 2030): in compliance as of Dec 31, 2025; **subsequent event** — a technical default occurred on Feb 2, 2026 when xAI was acquired (debt assumed at subsidiary level); cured by a covenant waiver and amendment on March 2, 2026. (p. F-30)
- **X 2027 and X 2030 Notes:** in compliance as of Dec 31, 2025. (p. F-31)
- **X First Lien Senior Credit Facilities (B-1 and B-3 Term Loans):** in compliance as of Dec 31, 2025. (p. F-31)
- **xAI Fixed Rate and Floating Rate Term Loans:** in compliance as of Dec 31, 2025. (p. F-32)
- **xAI 12.5% Senior Secured Notes:** in compliance as of Dec 31, 2025. (p. F-33)
- **SpaceX ABL Credit Agreement (old $1.5B revolver):** terminated in Feb 2025 with no amounts outstanding. (p. F-30)
- **xAI Revolving Line of Credit ($150M):** no borrowings in 2025; $145M of letters of credit issued as of Dec 31, 2025. (p. F-33)

### 4b. Prepayment penalties (make-whole and exit fees)

- **X B-1 Term Loan:** no prepayment premium; mandatory quarterly amortization of 0.25% of original principal + standard mandatory prepayments on debt issuances, asset sales, and excess cash flow sweeps (none triggered as of 12/31/25). (p. F-31)
- **X B-3 Term Loan:** 107.13% (pre-Oct 2026) → 104.75% (pre-Oct 2027) → 102.38% (pre-Oct 2028) of outstanding principal. (p. F-31)
- **xAI Fixed Rate and Floating Rate Term Loans:** 103% (pre-Jun 2027) → 101% (pre-Jun 2028) of outstanding principal. (p. F-32)
- **xAI 12.5% Senior Secured Notes:** 106.25% (pre-Jul 2027) → 103.13% (pre-Jul 2028) of outstanding principal. (p. F-33)
- **X 2027 / X 2030 Notes:** make-whole premium if redeemed before Sep 15, 2027 / Dec 1, 2029 respectively. (p. F-31)

### 4c. Collateral

- **X First Lien Senior Credit Facilities:** first priority lien on substantially all assets of X and its subsidiaries (carrying amount of $42,132M as of Dec 31, 2025). (p. F-31)
- **xAI term loans and Senior Secured Notes:** guaranteed by X.AI Corp. and subsidiaries (AIQ Phase LLC, CTC Holding LLC, CTC, LLZ Build LLC, MZX). (p. F-32, F-33)
- **X 2027 and X 2030 Notes:** senior unsecured. (p. F-31)
- **SpaceX Credit Facility:** senior unsecured. (p. F-30)
- **Other financings:** generally collateralized by specific machinery and equipment. (p. F-33)

### 4d. Specific financial covenants — NOT disclosed

The S-1 describes the covenants as "customary financial covenants" and "customary reporting requirements" but does not state the specific ratios. The SpaceX ABL Credit Agreement (since terminated) had a 85% draw-trigger threshold before financial covenants applied.

## 6. What's NOT Disclosed in the Audited Balance Sheet and Debt Notes

- **Dec 31, 2023 balance sheet line items** (cash, debt, equity components) are **not in the audited Balance Sheet** — the balance sheet only shows Dec 31, 2025 and Dec 31, 2024. This is standard for an S-1 (only two years of balance sheet data are required).
- **Dec 31, 2023 debt detail** is **not in Note 10 or Note 11** — both notes only present Dec 31, 2025 and Dec 31, 2024 columns.
- **2023 cash position** is **not in the Balance Sheet**. It is, however, in the Cash Flow Statement (p. F-9, "Cash and cash equivalents and restricted cash, end of year" = $4,690M for 2023) — but that is outside the requested sources.
- **2023 total shareholders' equity ($3,951M)** is **not in the Balance Sheet** but is in the Statement of Shareholders' Equity (p. F-7). Flagged in the table.
- **Specific covenant ratios** (e.g., interest coverage, leverage caps, minimum liquidity) — **not disclosed**. The S-1 only states that covenants are "customary" and that the company is in compliance.
- **Net debt as a defined metric** — **not disclosed** by the company; the line "Total debt and finance leases" in Note 10 is the closest disclosed subtotal.
- **Debt to equity ratio** — **not disclosed** as a headline metric in the S-1. The audited statements do not compute it.

**Read:** Total debt + leases / equity ratio jumped from 3.17x (2024) to 9.50x (2025), but almost entirely because **shareholders' equity fell from $4.86B to $2.57B** (driven by the $4.9B 2025 net loss) rather than because debt grew proportionally. Total funded debt + leases grew 58% YoY, but equity shrank 47% YoY.

On a **net debt** basis, SpaceX went from $4.0B net debt in 2024 to **net cash of $0.3B** in 2025 — debt grew faster than cash in absolute terms, but cash grew faster than debt (cash more than doubled). The company is **not** a leveraged story in 2025; it is a heavily capital-intensive story being funded by a mix of debt and equity issuance (the equity statement shows $17.9B of preferred stock issued in 2025 and $2.1B of other equity inflows).

## 8. Reconciliation Note (What Did Not Reconcile on the Second Pass)

No discrepancies. All figures reconfirmed on the second independent read:

- Balance sheet totals (p. F-4): cash $24,747M / $11,385M; debt and finance leases (current + non-current) $22,896M / $13,793M; total equity $2,573M / $4,863M. ✓
- Note 10 totals (p. F-30): Total debt and finance leases (net) $22,896M / $13,793M — **ties exactly to the Balance Sheet** line. ✓
- Note 11 lease totals (p. F-35): finance lease liabilities $1,237M / $1,531M — **ties exactly to Note 10 finance lease line** ($1,237M / $1,531M). ✓
- Statement of Shareholders' Equity (p. F-7): total equity balances $2,573M / $4,863M / $3,951M — 2024 and 2025 tie to Balance Sheet; 2023 is balance-sheet-absent but appears in the equity rollforward. ✓
- Maturity schedule (p. F-33): sum of all years = $22,049M = total principal in Note 10. ✓

The only thing worth flagging that is **not** a numerical discrepancy but an interpretive one: the S-1 does not state whether the operating lease liabilities ($1,558M as of Dec 31, 2025) are included in the company's headline definition of "total debt." Note 10's subtotal ("Total debt and finance leases") explicitly excludes operating leases; the user asked for "total debt including finance and operating leases," so I added them. Different analysts will treat operating leases differently; the figures above show both views.
