# SpaceX S-1: Cash Flow & Profitability — Verified

**Source:** Space Exploration Technologies Corp. Form S-1, audited consolidated financial statements (PCAOB-audited) for fiscal years ended December 31, 2025, 2024, 2023.

**Method:** Income statement and cash flow statement re-read from scratch on second pass; each figure checked against the MD&A "Summary of Cash flows" table and the Note 19 "Supplemental segment information" capex line.

**Headline:** SpaceX generated positive operating cash flow in all three years, but capex overwhelmed CFO in 2024 and 2025. **Free cash flow was positive in 2023 ($105M) and negative in 2024 ($-5,387M) and 2025 ($-13,952M).** The company is, on this evidence, a cash burner on a post-capex basis in 2024–2025 despite reporting positive earnings in 2024.

**Citations (exact):**
- Net income (loss): S-1, Consolidated Statements of Operations, p. F-5
- Cash from operations: S-1, Consolidated Statements of Cash Flows, p. F-8 (line: "Net cash provided by operating activities")
- CapEx: S-1, Consolidated Statements of Cash Flows, p. F-8 (line: "Purchases of property and equipment, net" — net of proceeds from disposals of $666M in 2025, $171M in 2024, $11M in 2023, per parenthetical on the same line)
- Free cash flow: derived as CFO − CapEx; **not a line item disclosed in the S-1**

**Reconciliation note:** 2023 capex of $4,415M is essentially equal to 2023 CFO of $4,520M, which is why FCF is only +$105M despite a $4.6B accounting net loss — the loss is dominated by a $3,775M non-cash intangible-asset impairment (Twitter brand), not operating cash burn.

## 2. Segment-Level Disclosure (Note 19 – Segments, p. F-58 to F-59)

The S-1 **does not break out net income, CFO, or FCF by segment.** It does disclose segment **capital expenditures** in the "Supplemental segment information" block at the bottom of each year's segment table. It also discloses segment **income (loss) from operations** in the body of each table.

## 5. Reconciliation Note (What Did Not Reconcile on the Second Pass)

No discrepancies. All nine figures (3 net income + 3 CFO + 3 CapEx) and the nine segment-CapEx figures reconfirmed exactly on the second independent read. Page citations verified by cross-referencing the table of contents (Index to Financial Statements, p. F-1) and the page footers in the body of the file.

The only material interpretive point worth flagging: the cash flow statement reports **net** capex (purchases minus disposal proceeds); the segment-CapEx table in Note 19 also reports the same net figure. The S-1 does not separately disclose gross PP&E additions, but it does disclose "Acquisition of property, plant, and equipment included in accounts payable" of $7,088M (2025), $2,481M (2024), $505M (2023) on p. F-9 — a noncash supplemental disclosure. If you add this to net capex, you get the gross change in PP&E: 2025 ~$27.8B, 2024 ~$13.6B, 2023 ~$4.9B.
