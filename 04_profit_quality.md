# SpaceX S-1: Profit Quality — Net Income vs. Cash from Operations — Verified

**Source:** Space Exploration Technologies Corp. Form S-1, audited consolidated financial statements (PCAOB-audited) for fiscal years ended December 31, 2025, 2024, 2023.

**Method:** Re-read the Consolidated Statements of Operations (p. F-5) and Consolidated Statements of Cash Flows (p. F-8 to F-9) from scratch on the second pass. Walked through the full reconciliation from net income to cash from operations, line by line.

**Headline:** **Yes, the reported profit is real in 2024.** The 2024 net income of $791M is fully backed by $5,776M of operating cash flow — a 7.3x coverage ratio. In 2023 and 2025, the company reported **net losses** that are not real cash burn: 2023's $(4,628)M net loss became +$4,520M of operating cash flow (the Twitter brand impairment was a $3,775M non-cash charge), and 2025's $(4,937)M net loss became +$6,785M of operating cash flow. **Across all three years, SpaceX has been a strong cash generator relative to reported profit.**

## 3. The Largest Items Driving the Gap

### 3a. Depreciation and amortization (the biggest single add-back every year)

| Year | D&A ($M) | Page |
|---|---:|---|
| 2025 | 6,701 | p. F-8 |
| 2024 | 3,824 | p. F-8 |
| 2023 | 2,635 | p. F-8 |

D&A roughly doubled in 2024 vs 2023 (largely Starlink satellite constellation) and grew another 75% in 2025 (largely AI data center build-out, per Note 19 segment D&A of $757M Space / $2,376M Connectivity / $3,568M AI in 2025, p. F-58). This is the dominant reason CFO exceeds net income — the company is depreciating a large and growing asset base.

### 3b. Stock-based compensation (non-cash, growing fast)

| Year | SBC ($M) | Page |
|---|---:|---|
| 2025 | 1,947 | p. F-8 |
| 2024 | 784 | p. F-8 |
| 2023 | 679 | p. F-8 |

SBC more than doubled in 2025 vs 2024. Note 19 (Segments, p. F-58) shows the 2025 SBC was split $515M Space / $369M Connectivity / $1,063M AI, with the AI segment carrying the most of the increase. This is a real economic cost to shareholders (dilution) but is not a cash outflow.

### 3c. The 2023 Twitter brand impairment — the single most important one-time item

| Year | Intangible asset impairment ($M) | Page |
|---|---:|---|
| 2025 | — | p. F-8 |
| 2024 | — | p. F-8 |
| 2023 | 3,775 | p. F-8 |

This $3,775M non-cash charge — recognized following the Twitter-to-X rebrand — drove 41% of the 2023 net loss. It is also reflected as a separate "Impairment" line of $3,775M in the 2023 income statement (p. F-5), so the audited statements do report it transparently. Without this charge, 2023 net loss would have been $(853)M instead of $(4,628)M. It is a real economic loss (the brand value disappeared) but a non-cash item.

### 3d. Deferred income taxes (the source of a 2025 "fake" tax expense)

| Year | Deferred income taxes ($M, add-back) | Total tax provision / (benefit) per P&L ($M) | Cash income taxes paid ($M, supplemental) | Page |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---|
| 2025 | 626 | 718 | 154 | p. F-8, F-5, F-9 |
| 2024 | (675) | (549) | 134 | p. F-8, F-5, F-9 |
| 2023 | (409) | (363) | 45 | p. F-8, F-5, F-9 |

**2025 tax story:** The income statement shows a $718M tax *expense*, but the company only paid $154M in cash for income taxes. The $626M deferred component is the non-cash add-back that helps net income converge to operating cash flow. **2024 tax story:** A $549M *benefit* (positive for net income) that is largely a deferred-tax accounting adjustment — driven by the release of a valuation allowance on deferred tax assets, **not** a cash refund. Cash taxes paid in 2024 were still $134M. **2023:** Same pattern — a $363M benefit, cash taxes paid of only $45M.

The deferred tax swings are large, non-cash, and recur every year. They are the second-largest "explanation" for the gap after D&A.

The 2025 reversal of $112M (a loss) cuts the other direction. These are clearly marked in the cash flow statement as "Unrealized (gain) loss on digital assets."

### 3g. Restructuring charges (in P&L, paid in cash — *not* a reconciling item, but worth flagging)

| Year | Restructuring charges ($M, P&L) | Page |
|---|---:|---|
| 2025 | 487 | p. F-5 |
| 2024 | 213 | p. F-5 |
| 2023 | 237 | p. F-5 |

These are *real cash charges* (severance, lease termination costs). Note 20 (Restructuring, p. F-60) shows $487M, $147M, and $77M of severance/termination benefits for 2025/24/23, plus $36M (2024) and $54M (2023) of operating-lease ROU impairments (a non-cash portion). Unlike impairments and D&A, restructuring does **not** get added back in the cash flow reconciliation — it is treated as an operating cash outflow because severance and lease termination fees are paid in cash. So restructuring is *not* a driver of the gap between net income and CFO; it is paid in cash and is already in the operating activities.

### 3h. Working capital — the second-largest contributor to the gap (after non-cash items)

Working capital changes added **+$2,089M (2025) / +$1,673M (2024) / +$2,006M (2023)** of cash. The biggest single line is **deferred revenue** ($1,929M / $1,876M / $1,695M) — customers paying upfront for Starlink subscriptions, Aviation Wi-Fi, and Launch & Development contracts, with revenue recognized over time. This is a real, recurring source of operating cash. **Accounts payable** growth ($709M / $472M / $220M) is also a meaningful source — the company is stretching supplier terms as it scales.

**Not a reconciling item in the technical sense** — these are real cash flows — but the scale of the deferred-revenue buildup is what makes the CFO figure larger than the net income figure would otherwise suggest.

## 4. Summary: Why Net Income and CFO Diverge

| Driver | 2025 | 2024 | 2023 |
|---|---:|---:|---:|
| **Non-cash D&A** (largest single add-back) | +6,701 | +3,824 | +2,635 |
| **Non-cash SBC** | +1,947 | +784 | +679 |
| **Non-cash Twitter brand impairment** (2023 only) | — | — | +3,775 |
| **Non-cash deferred tax expense / (benefit)** | +626 | (675) | (409) |
| **Non-cash digital-asset mark** | +112 | (955) | — |
| **Other non-cash items** (impairment, debt amort, other) | +247 | +334 | +462 |
| **Working capital generation (mainly deferred revenue + AP)** | +2,089 | +1,673 | +2,006 |
| **Total: bridge from net income to CFO** | **+11,722** | **+4,985** | **+9,148** |

**The honest answer to "is the profit real?":**
- **2024 profit ($791M): real**, and the underlying operating performance is *much better* than the headline — the company generated $5,776M of cash, so the small GAAP profit understates the cash engine.
- **2023 and 2025 losses: the losses are real economically** (shareholders' equity fell in both years — see file 03) **but the cash position is strong** — the company is generating billions in operating cash despite reporting large GAAP losses. The losses are dominated by non-cash charges (D&A, impairments, deferred taxes).

In all three years, **operating cash flow is a much better measure of underlying performance than net income**, and operating cash flow is strongly positive and growing ($4.5B → $5.8B → $6.8B).

## 5. Items NOT Disclosed in the Audited Income Statement / Cash Flow Statement

- **Composition of the "Other" non-cash add-back line** ($66M / $115M / $214M): not itemized in the audited statements; presumably includes items like amortization of other intangibles, P&L impacts of certain equity awards, and similar non-cash items. The S-1 does not break it out in the audited cash flow statement.
- **Stock-based compensation by income statement line** (e.g., SBC in cost of revenue vs. R&D vs. SG&A): the S-1 does not disclose this in the audited statements; only the total SBC add-back in the cash flow and the segment SBC in Note 19 (p. F-58) are disclosed.
- **2025 tax-expense detail** (e.g., the One Big Beautiful Bill Act impact is discussed in the MD&A on p. 114–115, but specific dollar impact is **not disclosed** in the audited statements).
- **Specific deferred-tax-asset valuation allowance movements**: the 2024 release is referenced in the MD&A but not separately quantified in the audited income statement or cash flow statement.
